Our Process

Industrial Valve Repair Procedures

Our systematic 9-step repair process ensures every valve is restored to peak performance.

Initial Inspection & Assessment

Every valve undergoes a thorough inspection to identify wear, corrosion, leakage, or operational issues through visual inspection, functional testing, and identification of damaged components.

1
2

Valve Dismantling

The valve is carefully disassembled using proper tools and techniques to avoid further damage. Safe removal from pipeline, marking of components for correct reassembly, and controlled dismantling process.

Cleaning & Surface Preparation

All parts are cleaned to remove deposits, rust, and old sealing materials through chemical cleaning/degreasing, sandblasting or wire brushing, and surface preparation for inspection.

3
4

Detailed Component Inspection

Each component is checked for defects and dimensional accuracy. Seat and sealing surface inspection, stem, disc, and body evaluation, and measurement against standards.

Repair & Refurbishment

Damaged parts are repaired or replaced to restore valve performance. Lapping of sealing surfaces, machining (lathe, milling, drilling), welding and metal build-up, and rubber lining / seat replacement.

5
6

Reassembly

The valve is reassembled with precision to ensure proper alignment and sealing. Use of new gaskets and seals, torque-controlled tightening, and alignment verification.

Testing & Quality Assurance

Each valve is tested to meet industry standards and ensure reliability through hydrostatic pressure testing, leakage testing, and functional operation testing.

7
8

Painting & Finishing

Final finishing improves durability and appearance with anti-corrosion coating, industrial-grade painting, and tagging and documentation.

Final Inspection & Dispatch

Before delivery, the valve undergoes final checks and proper packaging. Quality certification, documentation report, and safe packing for transport.

Maintenance Procedure

1

Pre-Maintenance Checks

  • Isolate equipment and follow LOTO (Lockout/Tagout)
  • Ensure system is depressurized and drained
  • Wear proper PPE (gloves, helmet, safety shoes)
  • Review previous maintenance records
2

Visual Inspection

Pump Inspection
  • Check for leakage, vibration, noise
  • Inspect seals, coupling, and piping connections
Gearbox Inspection
  • Check oil level and condition
  • Inspect for overheating, abnormal sound, leakage
3

Disassembly

  • Mark parts for correct reassembly
  • Remove casing, shaft, bearings, seals carefully
  • Drain oil (gearbox) and fluid (pump)
4

Cleaning

  • Clean all components using solvent/degreaser
  • Remove dirt, sludge, and deposits
  • Dry using compressed air
5

Repair / Replacement

  • Replace damaged bearings, seals, gaskets, O-ring
  • Repair or replace worn gears/impeller
  • Ensure correct tolerances and fits
6

Reassembly

  • Reassemble as per markings and alignment
  • Apply proper lubrication
  • Tighten bolts to recommended torque
7

Alignment & Lubrication

  • Align pump with motor (dial gauge/laser)
  • Fill gearbox with recommended oil
  • Ensure correct coupling alignment
8

Testing & Commissioning

  • Perform no-load trial run
  • Check vibration, noise, temperature
  • Verify flow, pressure (pump)
  • Ensure no oil or fluid leakage

Rubber Solutions

Rubber Moulding Machine for Valve O-Ring Manufacturing

Rubber moulding machines are specialized industrial systems designed to produce high-precision O-rings and sealing components used in valves, pumps, pipelines, and critical industrial equipment. These machines transform raw elastomer materials into durable, leak-proof seals through controlled heat, pressure, and moulding processes.

Machine Components

  • O-rings and sealing rings
  • Gaskets (flat, spiral, custom shapes)
  • Oil seals and mechanical seals
  • Rubber bushes and dampers
  • Valve seats and liners
  • Rubber rollers and bellows

Valve Industry Components

  • Valve seats (soft/hard seated)
  • PTFE / rubber lined sleeves
  • O-rings and sealing kits
  • Gland packing components
  • Rubber disc and seat rings
